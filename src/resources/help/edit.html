Edit Window

Choosing to edit a Song or Custom Slide will bring up an editing window where you can make changes to that item's title, lyrics, text, etc.

Song Editor Elements

To the very top of the editing window you will find a Title line. This will be the title of your song. The next three lines will be for the Author, any Copyright information associated with the song, and the number CCLI has assigned to this song (if the song comes from CCLI).

Next is the lyrics box. In here are the lyric blocks of your song. Lyric blocks are the individual parts of your song. For example, Verse 1, Verse 2, Chorus 1, and Ending 1. You can add a block of lyrics by clicking the Add Lyrics button. Doing so will create a new lyrics block where you can specify the type of lyrics (Verse, Chorus, Bridge, etc.), the number, (Verse 1, Verse 2, etc.), and the lyrics themselves. Basic formatting functions are available for your text, namely Bold, Italic, and Underline.

Your next step will be to specify the order in which you want verses, choruses, etc. to be displayed. To the right of the lyrics, you will see a box labeled Song Order. Simply drag-and-drop the lyric blocks you have created in their correct order in the Song Order box. You can rearrange any of the items in the Song Order box by dragging them up and down, and if you need to remove a segment from the song's order, click on it then press the Delete button on your keyboard.

It might be more convenient to edit all the lyrics at once. For example, if you want to paste all the lyrics from your computer's clipboard. To do so, click the Edit All Lyrics toggle button. The editor changes to a view that shows all lyrics you currently have, as well as a row of buttons you can use to insert the tags that will identify what each part of your lyrics are (Verse, Chorus, etc.). Whatever text is below the tag you insert belongs to that tag. Once you are satisfied with how you've edited the lyrics, click the Edit All Lyrics toggle button again and the lyrics box will divide up your lyrics according to your inserted tags.

Custom Slide Editor Elements

The editor for custom slides is much simpler. At the top is the title you will give to your custom slide. Below that is the area in which you will enter the text you want to appear on the slide.

Note: if you would like your custom slide to be split into multiple slides, insert a blank line between the text you want on each slide. Then, select the "Split Slides" button (more info below).

Custom Slide Settings

By default, your songs and custom slides will use the font and background you've selected for songs and bible passages, respectively. However, you can apply a custom background and font setting to your songs or custom slides. To use these custom settings, select the Custom Slide Settings button in the editor. This will open these custom settings in the editor.

By default, a song will display a footer showing the song's title, composer, any copyright info, and your CCLI license number, if you have one. To prevent this footer from showing, uncheck the box beside "Use Footer for this song." This option is not available for custom slides as they have no footer.

From here, you can go on to specify the font you want to use, its size, and its color. You can also change the settings for the font's shadow, outline, and background color.

Additionally, you can choose which background you want your song or custom slide to use. You can choose to use whatever background is set for songs or bible passages, a solid color, or any of the backgrounds you've already added to ProjectOn.

Additional Custom Slide Settings

For custom slides, you also have to additional options: Add Audio and Split Slides.

Selecting the Add Audio button will allow you to choose an audio file that will automatically play when the slide is shown. Choose an audio file you've already imported, or select a new audio file by clicking on the folder icon. If you want the audio to loop back to the beginning and play again when it's done, select the Loop button.

Selecting the Split Slides button will split your slide text into individual slides wherever you have inserted a blank line. When you select this button, you are given the option to auto-play the individual slides. Checking the Auto-Play Slide Text box means that the individual slides will be cycled through automatically, showing each individual slide for the amount of time you've set in the Secs. Between Slides box.

Save and Cancel

Finally, you have the Save and Cancel buttons. Press "Save" to save your changes, or press "Cancel" to undo any changes you have made. Using the "X" at the top-right of the window is the same as pressing "Cancel."
